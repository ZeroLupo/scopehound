About

Built because competitive intel shouldn't cost a fortune or live in a silo.

ScopeHound was born at a startup where everyone needed to know what competitors were doing, but nobody had time to check. So I put the intel where the team already lived: Slack.

The Origin

ScopeHound started as an internal tool. While helping build Alfie.io, an AI-powered affiliate marketing platform, I realized I was spending hours manually checking competitor websites, Reddit threads, Product Hunt launches, and Hacker News posts for pricing changes, new features, and market moves.

The enterprise tools that did this cost thousands per month and locked insights inside dashboards nobody checked. I needed something leaner: automated monitoring that delivered analyzed intel directly into Slack, where decisions actually happen.

So I built it. Then I open-sourced it, because every startup deserves access to competitive intelligence, not just the ones with enterprise budgets.

"I didn't need a Michelin-star intelligence platform. I needed a diner that serves reliable intel, fast, at a price that makes sense."
